CROUP CLINICAL PATHWAY
Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario (CHEO)

DEFINITION
Croup is a viral illness causing laryngotracheal inflammation. Clinical hallmarks: barky cough, hoarseness, inspiratory stridor.

SEVERITY ASSESSMENT
CHEO uses Westley Croup Score (same parameters as international standard):
Score 0-2: Mild | Score 3-7: Moderate | Score 8+: Severe

NOTE ON CHEO DOSING
CHEO guideline uses a single higher dexamethasone dose across mild-moderate-severe croup, per more recent evidence favouring higher dosing for consistency and efficacy.

MANAGEMENT

MILD (Westley 0-2):
- Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg oral, single dose, max 10mg
- Discharge home with return-to-ED instructions

MODERATE (Westley 3-7):
- Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg oral, single dose, max 10mg
- Observe minimum 2 hours post-dose
- Reassess severity score every 30 minutes
- Nebulised epinephrine 0.5 mg/kg (max 5mg) of 1:1000 if stridor at rest persists

SEVERE (Westley 8+):
- Dexamethasone 0.6 mg/kg PO/IV/IM, single dose, max 10mg
- Nebulised epinephrine 0.5 mg/kg (max 5mg) 1:1000 IMMEDIATELY
- Supplemental O2 if SpO2 <92%
- Notify senior physician, PICU consult
- Minimum 4 hours observation

DISCHARGE CRITERIA
- No stridor at rest for >2 hours post-intervention
- No respiratory distress
- Adequate oral intake
- SpO2 ≥94% on room air
- Parents/caregivers able to recognize deterioration

DANGEROUS DIFFERENTIALS
Rule out epiglottitis, bacterial tracheitis, foreign body aspiration, retropharyngeal abscess before treating as croup.
