HYPERTENSION IN ADULTS — NICE NG136
National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (UK)

DIAGNOSIS
Confirm with ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) or home BP monitoring (HBPM).

Stage 1: Clinic BP ≥140/90 and ABPM/HBPM ≥135/85
Stage 2: Clinic BP ≥160/100 and ABPM/HBPM ≥150/95
Stage 3 (severe): Clinic systolic ≥180 OR diastolic ≥120

TREATMENT THRESHOLDS
Offer drug treatment to:
- Stage 2 hypertension (any age)
- Stage 1 hypertension with target organ damage, established CVD, renal disease, diabetes, or 10-year CVD risk ≥10%
- Age <60 with stage 1 hypertension — consider drug treatment

FIRST-LINE (Stage 1 under age 55, NOT Afro-Caribbean)
- ACE inhibitor (ramipril, lisinopril, enalapril)
- OR ARB (candesartan, losartan) if ACEi not tolerated (cough)

FIRST-LINE (Age ≥55 OR Afro-Caribbean any age)
- Calcium channel blocker (amlodipine, felodipine)
- OR thiazide-like diuretic (indapamide) if CCB not tolerated

SECOND-LINE (if not controlled)
- Add whichever first-line you didn't start with (ACEi/ARB + CCB combination)

THIRD-LINE
- Add thiazide-like diuretic

FOURTH-LINE (resistant hypertension)
- If K ≤4.5: add spironolactone 25mg daily
- If K >4.5: add alpha-blocker or beta-blocker
- Specialist referral

TARGETS
- Under 80: <140/90 (clinic), <135/85 (ABPM/HBPM)
- 80 and over: <150/90 (clinic), <145/85 (ABPM/HBPM)

SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS BY DRUG

ACE inhibitors / ARBs:
- Contraindicated in pregnancy (teratogenic)
- Caution in bilateral renal artery stenosis
- Check U&Es, eGFR before starting and within 2 weeks of starting/titration
- Hyperkalaemia risk — monitor K+

Calcium channel blockers:
- Caution in heart failure (avoid non-dihydropyridines like verapamil, diltiazem)
- Ankle oedema common side effect

Thiazide-like diuretics:
- Hyponatraemia, hypokalaemia risk
- Gout risk
- Caution in severe renal impairment

NSAIDs + antihypertensives:
NSAIDs reduce effectiveness of ACE inhibitors, ARBs, diuretics, beta-blockers. Increase renal injury risk when combined with ACEi/ARB + diuretic (triple whammy). Advise paracetamol for analgesia instead where possible.
