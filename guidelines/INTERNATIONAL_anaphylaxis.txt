ANAPHYLAXIS — EMERGENCY MANAGEMENT
International consensus (WAO, ASCIA, Resuscitation Council UK aligned)

DEFINITION
Acute, potentially life-threatening systemic hypersensitivity reaction. Diagnose clinically — do NOT delay treatment for tests.

DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA (any one)
1. Acute onset of illness involving skin/mucosal tissue AND either respiratory compromise OR hypotension
2. Two or more of: skin/mucosal involvement, respiratory compromise, hypotension, persistent GI symptoms after likely allergen exposure
3. Hypotension after exposure to known allergen for that patient

IMMEDIATE MANAGEMENT — TIME CRITICAL

STEP 1: CALL FOR HELP
- Hospital: activate MET/code blue
- Community: call emergency services (000/911/999/112)
- DO NOT leave the patient

STEP 2: ADRENALINE (EPINEPHRINE) — FIRST LINE, IMMEDIATELY
Route: INTRAMUSCULAR — anterolateral thigh (vastus lateralis)
NEVER subcutaneous. IV only by experienced clinicians in monitored setting.

ADULT (≥12 years or ≥50kg):
- Adrenaline 500 micrograms IM (0.5mg = 0.5ml of 1:1000)

CHILD 6-12 years (approx 25-50kg):
- Adrenaline 300 micrograms IM (0.3mg = 0.3ml of 1:1000)

CHILD 6 months - 6 years (approx 7.5-25kg):
- Adrenaline 150 micrograms IM (0.15mg = 0.15ml of 1:1000)

INFANT <6 months (<7.5kg):
- Adrenaline 100-150 micrograms IM (0.1-0.15ml of 1:1000)
- OR 10 micrograms/kg if weight known

REPEAT adrenaline every 5 minutes if no improvement.

STEP 3: POSITIONING
- Supine with legs elevated (if hypotensive)
- Sitting upright ONLY if respiratory distress prominent
- Left lateral if pregnant
- DO NOT stand patient up (risk of empty ventricle syndrome — sudden cardiac arrest)

STEP 4: HIGH-FLOW OXYGEN
- 15L/min via non-rebreather mask

STEP 5: IV ACCESS + FLUID RESUSCITATION
- If hypotensive: IV crystalloid bolus 20ml/kg (paediatric) or 500-1000ml (adult) rapidly
- Repeat as needed

ADJUNCTS (AFTER adrenaline — not first-line)
- Antihistamines: H1 blocker (cetirizine oral or chlorphenamine IV)
  NOTE: No evidence for efficacy in anaphylaxis. Only for urticaria/itching after primary treatment.
- Corticosteroids: Hydrocortisone 200mg IV adult, 4mg/kg paediatric
  NOTE: No evidence reduces biphasic reactions. Not first-line.
- Bronchodilators: Salbutamol nebulised if bronchospasm persists after adrenaline

MONITORING
- Minimum 6-8 hours observation post-reaction
- Biphasic reactions occur in ~5% of cases
- Admit if severe, ongoing symptoms, or multiple adrenaline doses required

DISCHARGE
- Adrenaline autoinjector prescription (EpiPen, Anapen, or equivalent)
- Patient education on autoinjector use
- Allergy clinic referral
- Written anaphylaxis action plan
- ASCIA or regional equivalent patient resources

CONTRAINDICATIONS TO ADRENALINE
NONE in anaphylaxis. Adrenaline is first-line regardless of cardiac history. Withholding adrenaline in anaphylaxis is more dangerous than giving it.
