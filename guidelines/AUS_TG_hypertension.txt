HYPERTENSION IN ADULTS — THERAPEUTIC GUIDELINES AUSTRALIA
Australian Heart Foundation aligned

DIAGNOSIS
Confirm sustained elevation with repeat clinic measurements, ABPM, or HBPM.

Grade 1: 140-159/90-99 mmHg
Grade 2: 160-179/100-109 mmHg
Grade 3: ≥180/110 mmHg

Target: <140/90 for most adults. <130/80 for high cardiovascular risk, diabetes, CKD.

TREATMENT INITIATION
Consider drug therapy alongside lifestyle modification when:
- Grade 2 or 3 hypertension
- Grade 1 with absolute CVD risk ≥10% over 5 years
- Grade 1 with end-organ damage

FIRST-LINE OPTIONS (any one of)
- ACE inhibitor (perindopril 4-8mg, ramipril 2.5-10mg)
- ARB (irbesartan 150-300mg, candesartan 8-32mg, telmisartan 40-80mg)
- Thiazide-like diuretic (indapamide 1.5mg SR or 2.5mg)
- Dihydropyridine CCB (amlodipine 2.5-10mg, felodipine 5-10mg)

Australian guidelines permit any of four classes as first-line, differing from UK NICE staged approach.

FIRST-LINE SELECTION BY PATIENT FACTORS
- Young patient without comorbidity: ACEi or ARB
- Older patient (>65): CCB or thiazide-like diuretic
- Isolated systolic hypertension: CCB or thiazide
- Diabetes with proteinuria: ACEi or ARB (renal protection)
- Heart failure: ACEi/ARB + beta-blocker

COMBINATION THERAPY
If single agent inadequate at maximum tolerated dose:
- ACEi or ARB + CCB
- ACEi or ARB + thiazide-like diuretic
- CCB + thiazide-like diuretic

Avoid ACEi + ARB combination (increased adverse effects without benefit).

RESISTANT HYPERTENSION
Three agents at maximum tolerated doses including a diuretic:
- Add spironolactone 25-50mg daily
- Refer to cardiology/hypertension specialist

SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

ACE inhibitors / ARBs:
- Pregnancy Category D — contraindicated in pregnancy
- Avoid with potassium supplements, potassium-sparing diuretics
- Check U&Es, creatinine before and within 1-2 weeks of initiation
- Dry cough common with ACEi (10-15%) — switch to ARB

NSAIDs:
- Reduce antihypertensive effect of all classes
- Increased renal injury risk especially with ACEi/ARB + diuretic combinations
- Advise paracetamol first-line for analgesia

Beta-blockers:
- No longer routinely first-line unless specific indication (IHD, heart failure, AF)
- Avoid in asthma
